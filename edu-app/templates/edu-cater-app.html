About edu-cater

edu-cater is an application that uses topic modeling (Latent Dirichlet Allocation) and graph theory to provide customized curriculums of courses to users to ease their transition to new skills sets of interest. More information about the project can be found here.

To use educater, explore the topics below. Using the dropdown menus, select a topic that you are familiar with, and a new topic that you wish to learn. You can also rate the importance of course similarity, course ratings, course popularity (based on enrollment) and course length (where higher weights correspond to shorter course lengths).

The "Course Network" provides a thresholded visual representation of the graph that is used for the course curriculum recommendations. Each node represents a course, and each edge (connection) represents the similarity between two courses in terms of how they relate to the different topics. In the graph that is used for recommendations, the edges are thresholded such that only the top 7.5% of connections are kept. For the visualization provided on this page, the graph was thresholded and binarized to show the top 2.5% of connections. Courses with a node strength less than 5 were then discarded.
